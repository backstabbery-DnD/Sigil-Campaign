## The Decrees of the Lady (The Absolute Laws)

The Lady of Pain does not care about street crime, theft, or local brawls. She has only a few rules, communicated silently through her floating servants, the [[Dabus]]. If you break any of these, there is no trial, no defense barrister, and no prison cell—she will either slice you to ribbons with her shadow or banish you to a torturous, inescapable pocket dimension called the _Mazes_ forever.

- **1. No Divine Entities Allowed:** Gods, deities, and greater powers are strictly forbidden from entering Sigil. Their avatars cannot cross the threshold, and they cannot warp portals to force their way in. Sigil is entirely neutral ground.

- **2. No Worship of the Lady:** It is a capital crime to worship the Lady of Pain as a goddess. She despises devotion. Any cults that pop up trying to pray to her or build her shrines are ruthlessly and bloodily wiped out by her blades.

- **3. Do Not Harm the Dabus:** The Dabus are the caretakers of Sigil, cutting back razorvine and maintaining the city's infrastructure. Harming, attacking, or interfering with a Dabus is treated as a direct attack on the Lady herself.

- **4. Do Not Disrupt the Stability of the City:** Any action that threatens the fundamental existence or structural stability of Sigil—such as attempting to permanently seal all the portals, breaking the city torus, or sparking a full-scale war between planar armies in the streets—draws her immediate, fatal gaze.

- **5. The Faction Cap:** The Lady decrees that there may be no more than **15 active factions** operating within the city at any given time to keep the political balance from shattering into absolute chaos.


## The Mortal Laws

Everything else is managed by the Triad of Law (_The Harmonium, The Fraternity of Order,_ and _The Mercykillers_). These laws are passed by a majority vote of the faction representatives inside the **Hall of Speakers**. Because it is a city full of demons, angels, and cutthroats, the mortal legal system is intensely bureaucratic and heavily enforced.

### Crimes Against the City & State

- **Treason / Sabotage:** Attempting to manipulate the city’s portals, revealing secret gate codes to enemy planar armies, or trying to block the city’s trade routes. **Punishment:** Execution by the Wyrm (a lingering, brutal public death reserved for traitors).

- **Tax Evasion:** Failing to pay the city’s flat **5% commerce tax** on all business transactions or the monthly income tax (10% or 3 coppers, whichever is greater). **Punishment:** Seizure of assets, heavy fines, and execution by the blade for high-ranking officials.

- **Forgery of Official Documents:** Faking a Guvner warrant, a courthouse manifest, or a high-up’s signature. **Punishment:** Immediate exile from Sigil.

- **Hampering Justice:** Bribing city guards, hiding fugitives from the Mercykillers, or lying under a Zone of Truth in court. **Punishment:** Hard labor for up to two weeks and fines up to 200 gold pieces.


### Crimes of Aggression & Property

- **Murder:** Killing a citizen without a sanctioned legal warrant. **Punishment:** Hanging or permanent life imprisonment in the Prison. (Note: Brawling is technically tolerated in bars, but the moment a body drops, the Harmonium moves in).

- **Illegal Slavery:** Pressing a citizen of Sigil into slavery or kidnapping folks from the streets. **Punishment:** Up to 10 years of hard labor in the slave pits or the Lower Planes. _(Slavery itself is legal in Sigil if the paperwork is filed properly by Lower Planar merchants or if you are transporting slaves through the city on a legal planar manifest—but poaching citizens illegally is a fast track to the gallows)._

- **Theft and Fencing:** Robbing a merchant or selling stolen goods in the Market Ward. **Punishment:** A fine exactly equal to the total value of the stolen goods, paid directly to the city treasury (with 10% split among the three factions of Law).

- **Vandalism & Arson:** Setting fire to structures or damaging municipal property. **Punishment:** Imprisonment up to one week, covering the exact cost of repairs, and an additional fine of up to 100 gold pieces.
