<h1>D&D 5e Class Focus: The Artificer</h1>

Core Concept
---
Artificers are masters of invention, magic, and mechanics. Unlike wizards who study pure theory or sorcerers who wield innate power, artificers view magic as a system to be engineered. They channel magical energy through everyday items, tools, and custom-built inventions—turning mundane objects into extraordinary gear.

Key Mechanics & Features
---
Tools as Focus: Artificers must use a tool kit (like Thieves' Tools, Tinker's Tools, or Smith's Tools) as their spellcasting focus. Their spells are described not as chanting gestures, but as deploying gadgets, mixing volatile chemicals, or activating runes.

Magical Infusions: The class’s signature feature. Artificers can temporarily turn ordinary items into magical ones. They can imbue armor with extra defense, weapons with accuracy, or recreate existing magic items (like a Bag of Holding or Goggles of Night).

The Right Tool for the Job: They can magically manifest any artisan’s tool kit they need out of thin air during a short rest, ensuring they are always prepared to build or disarm.

The Specialist Subclasses
---
An artificer's role changes dramatically based on their chosen field of study:

Alchemist: The mystic pharmacist. They mix volatile concussions, healing elixirs, and acid. They excel at support, healing, and utility.

Armorer: The magical engineer. They customize a suit of heavy plate armor into a high-tech exoskeleton. The armor can be configured for stealthy infiltration or heavy-tanking frontline defense.

Artillerist: The combat specialist. They construct magical eldrich cannons—which can walk on legs or be held like a wand—to blast enemies with fire, force, or protect allies with temporary hit points.

Battle Smith: The mechanical knight. They build a loyal, clockwork or homunculus beast called a Steel Defender to fight alongside them. They use their intelligence modifier for weapon attacks and act as protectors on the battlefield.
