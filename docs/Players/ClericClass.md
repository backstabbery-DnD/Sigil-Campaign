Core Concept
---
Clerics are the mortal representatives of the gods, acting as conduits for divine magic. Unlike wizards who memorize spells or warlocks who strike bargains, a cleric's power stems directly from their devotion and faith. They are versatile, armored spellcasters who excel at healing the wounded, buffing allies, and calling down holy (or unholy) wrath upon their enemies.

Key Mechanics & Features
---
Divine Domain: Chosen at 1st level, a cleric’s domain represents their deity’s specific portfolio (e.g., Life, War, Trickery). It dictates their bonus spells, armor proficiencies, and heavily alters their playstyle.

Channel Divinity: A pool of divine energy that can be expended to fuel unique magical effects. All clerics can use this to Turn Undead (forcing undead creatures to flee), while their specific domain unlocks secondary, powerful abilities.

Prepared Spellcasting: Clerics have access to their entire spell list and can swap out their prepared spells every time they finish a long rest, making them incredibly adaptable to changing situations.

Divine Domains (Popular Subclasses)
---
A cleric’s domain entirely shapes their role on the battlefield and in the party:

Life Domain: The ultimate healers. Their spells restore significantly more hit points than other classes, and they can heal multiple allies at once using their Channel Divinity.

War Domain: Holy knights who charge into the front lines. They gain proficiency with heavy armor and martial weapons, and can use their divine power to ensure their weapon strikes hit the mark.

Light Domain: Radiating holy fire. These clerics act more like offensive blasters, using spells like Fireball and Radiance of the Dawn to burn away darkness and impose disadvantage on enemy attacks.

Trickery Domain: Servants of mischievous or stealthy deities. They can grant allies advantage on stealth checks, create perfect illusory duplicates of themselves, and use misdirection to control the battlefield.
