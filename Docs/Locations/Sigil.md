<h1>Sigil: The City of Doors</h1>

Core Concept
---
Sigil is a bustling, planar metropolis located at the center of the Multiverse, floating atop the impossibly tall Infinite Spire in the Outlands. Shaped like a massive, hollow torus (donut), the city's streets line the inside curve, meaning looking up reveals the buildings and sky of the opposite side of town. It has no natural sun, moon, or sky—only an ambient luminescent smog that brightens and dims over a 24-hour cycle.

Key Features & Rules
---
• The Portals: Sigil's defining trait. Virtually anything shaped like an opening (a doorway, an arch, a picture frame, a sewer grate) can be a portal to anywhere in the multiverse. However, every portal requires a specific Portal Key to open—which can be an object, a specific action, a phrase, or even a thought.

• The Lady of Pain: The enigmatic, bladed ruler of Sigil. She does not govern daily life, but she enforces two absolute laws: No gods are allowed in Sigil, and no one may disrupt the peace of the city. Worshiping her is strictly forbidden and results in being flayed or banished to her inescapable Mazes.

• The Cage: A common nickname for the city. While you can travel anywhere via portals, you cannot leave by physical means (flying out of the ring just loops you back or drops you into the void), making it a gilded cage.

---

The Six Wards
---
1. The Clerk’s Ward: The bureaucratic heart of the city, filled with beautiful architecture, government buildings, and libraries. Home to the Hall of Speakers.

2. The Grand Bazaar / Market Ward: The financial hub where you can buy or sell goods from any plane in existence.

3. The Guildhall Ward: The center for artisans, craftsmen, and traders learning their crafts.

4. The Lady’s Ward: The wealthiest district, housing the city’s elite, high-end courts, and the Prison.

5. The Lower Ward: An industrial, smog-choked slum dominated by foundries, portals to the Lower Planes, and the Shattered Temple.

6. The Hive: The poorest, most dangerous, and chaotic ward. A sprawling slum where the desperate, the lawless, and the Heralds of Dust reside.

The Ascendant Factions (The Core Philosophies)
---
Sigil is run by organizations tied to specific beliefs about the multiverse. Here are the 12 primary factions:

• Athar (Defiers): They believe the "gods" are just powerful frauds who don't deserve worship. They seek a true, higher divine force. (HQ: Shattered Temple)

• Bleak Cabal (Bleakers): They believe life and the multiverse have absolutely no inherent meaning. They cope by practicing selfless charity and running orphanages. (HQ: The Gatehouse)

• Doomguard (Sinkers): They celebrate inevitable decay, entropy, and the final destruction of all things. (HQ: The Armory)

• Fated (Takers): Radical social Darwinists. They believe those with power have the right to take whatever they want, and survival is entirely up to the individual. (HQ: Hall of Records)

• Fraternity of Order (Guvners): They believe that the multiverse is governed by absolute laws. By learning and exploiting these rules, they can control reality. (HQ: High Courts)

• Hands of Havoc (Wreakers): Chaotic anarchists dedicated to tearing down corrupt systems, laws, and societies to achieve total personal freedom. (HQ: Various warehouses)

• Harmonium (Hardheads): Authoritarian peacekeepers who believe universal harmony can only be achieved if everyone conforms to their strict ideals—by force if necessary. (HQ: City Barracks)

• Heralds of Dust (Dusters): They believe everyone is already dead, and this current life is just a cruel waiting room before achieving the ultimate peace of "True Death." (HQ: The Mortuary)

• Mercykillers (Jailers): Obsessively devoted to the absolute letter of the law and punishing the guilty. Justice must be executed without an ounce of mercy. (HQ: The Prison)

• Mind’s Eye (Seekers): They believe that each individual has a spark of the divine and can achieve literal godhood through personal growth, experience, and creation. (HQ: Great Foundry)

• Society of Sensation (Sensates): They believe the ultimate truth can only be found by experiencing everything the multiverse has to offer through the senses. (HQ: Civic Festhall)

• Transcendent Order (Ciphers): They strive to achieve perfect harmony with the multiverse by acting entirely on instinct and intuition, bypassing thought altogether. (HQ: Great Gymnasium)

Things to Look For / Plot Hooks
---
• Faction Wars: The factions constantly vie for ideological and political control over the city's infrastructure.

• Accidental Portals: Characters might inadvertently trigger a portal just by walking through a doorway while holding a specific item (e.g., a copper coin or a dry leaf).

• The Dabus: The Lady of Pain’s silent, levitating servants who maintain the city. They communicate exclusively through floating visual rebuses (picture puzzles) above their heads.
